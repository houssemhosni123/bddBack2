Definition of Behavior-Driven Development (BDD)

Behavior-Driven Development (BDD) is a software development methodology that focuses on the expected behavior of a system from the perspective of its users. Unlike traditional methods that concentrate on unit tests, BDD emphasizes collaboration among developers, testers, and business experts from the early stages of the development process. This is achieved through specifications written in the form of scenarios that are understandable by all stakeholders.

Adapting Behavior-Driven Development (BDD)

BDD can be adapted to different contexts and scenarios by adjusting the level of detail in the specifications and selecting the tools and frameworks that best fit the technologies and skills of the teams.

BDD Process Steps

This image outlines the Behavior-Driven Development (BDD) process in several key steps:

  • Initial Conversation:
    • Actors: Product owner and users.
    • Action: They discuss what they need from the product.
  • Requirements Elaboration:
    • Actors: Product owner, developer, and tester.
    • Action: Together, they elaborate on the requirements based on the initial discussion.
  • Defining Scenarios:
    • Actors: Development team (product owner, developer, and tester).
    • Action: They define the requirements as structured scenarios in clear and understandable terms.
  • Guiding Developers:
    • Actors: Developers.
    • Action: The scenarios guide the developers and serve as the basis for automated tests.
  • Tester Utilization:
    • Actors: Testers.
    • Action: Testers use these scenarios as the basis for their tests to ensure that each feature meets the defined expectations.
